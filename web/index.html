The Semantic Shift of "Freedom"

Interactive exploration of how "freedom" changed meaning across 200 years, using word embeddings from four independent sources.

Freedom & Liberty: Divergence and Reconvergence

"Freedom" and "liberty" were once near-synonyms. Our embedding analysis shows they diverged steadily from the 1830s to the 1990s — then started reconverging in the 21st century.

Cosine similarity between "freedom" and "liberty" vectors. Higher = more similar meaning. Three independent sources shown.

The reconvergence
0.40 → 0.71

Freedom-liberty similarity hit a low of 0.40 in the 1970s (COHA), then rebounded to 0.71 by 2024 (GloVe) — nearly back to 1830s levels. This is consistent across all three data sources.

Freedom on the Constraint ↔ Agency Axis

We built a semantic axis from "constraint" words (slavery, bondage, servitude, oppression) to "agency" words (autonomy, choice, ability, capacity), then projected "freedom" onto it across time.

SemAxis projection: higher = more agency-like, lower = more constraint-like. Click words above to compare. SGNS = same method as COHA. GloVe = different method, directional only.

What this shows: Freedom doesn't have a strong directional trend on this axis (-0.02/century, R²=0.07). It oscillates within a narrow band. The GloVe data points (2014, 2024) swing wildly because GloVe and SGNS are different algorithms — treat them as directional indicators only.

Is Freedom Special? Control Word Comparison

To verify that freedom's behavior isn't just generic semantic drift, we compared its trajectory to other abstract nouns on the same axis.

SemAxis projections for freedom vs. control words. Most words show stronger directional trends than freedom.

Honest finding: Freedom's trend (-0.02/century) is weaker than most control words. "Power" and "authority" show strong agency-ward movement. "Democracy" and "justice" show strong constraint-ward movement. Freedom sits in the middle — it's semantically stable on this particular axis, even as other political concepts shift around it.

Methodology

Data Sources

Source Period Method Role
COHA HistWords 1830–2000 SGNS Primary historical series
Google Books HistWords 1800–1990 SGNS Cross-validation
Wikipedia 2008 word2vec ~2008 SGNS Modern extension (same method)
GloVe (Wikipedia+Gigaword) 2014, 2024 GloVe Modern extension (different method)

Alignment

All non-COHA embeddings were Procrustes-aligned to COHA 2000s using shared vocabulary (32K+ words). This orthogonal rotation preserves internal geometry while mapping into a common space. The GloVe→SGNS alignment introduces additional noise since the underlying algorithms differ.

SemAxis Construction

The constraint→agency axis was built using seed words expanded via nearest neighbors in COHA 1900 (midpoint). Axis is fixed across all decades — only the projected words move. Seed sensitivity analysis (100 trials, dropping 1 seed per pole) confirmed the direction is robust.
