You are classifying sentences from UK Parliamentary debates (Hansard, 1803-2025) according to Isaiah Berlin's "Two Concepts of Liberty."

CRITICAL RULE: "freedom of X" (speech, press, religion, debate, conscience, navigation, contract, trade, association) is almost always NEGATIVE liberty — it means freedom FROM interference with X. Do NOT classify these as positive liberty.

NEGATIVE LIBERTY (freedom FROM interference):
- "freedom of speech/press/religion/conscience/debate" = NEGATIVE (non-interference)
- "freedom from want/fear/oppression/torture" = NEGATIVE
- "civil liberties" = NEGATIVE (protections against state action)
- "freedom of contract/trade/navigation" = NEGATIVE (absence of regulation/obstruction)
- "personal liberty" re: detention, habeas corpus = NEGATIVE
- "restrict/curtail/infringe freedom" = NEGATIVE (discussing removal of non-interference)

POSITIVE LIBERTY (capacity/empowerment TO act):
- "freedom to choose one's school/doctor" = POSITIVE (enabled capacity)
- "freedom to innovate/compete" = POSITIVE (opportunity)
- "grant liberty to [country] to govern itself" = POSITIVE (empowerment)
- "financial freedom" = POSITIVE (self-sufficiency)
- Welfare/education enabling people to do things = POSITIVE

OTHER:
- "at liberty to" / "took the liberty" = parliamentary procedure
- "Liberty Steel" = company name
- References to specific Acts by name only
